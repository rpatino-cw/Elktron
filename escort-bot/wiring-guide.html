Voltage Divider — HC-SR04 ECHO

The ECHO pin outputs 5V but Pi 5 GPIO is 3.3V tolerant. Use a resistor divider to step it down:

ECHO pin ──┬── 100Ω ── GPIO 24 (Pi)
           └── 200Ω ── GND

Vout = 5V × 200/(100+200) = 3.33V ✓

Resistors on hand: 100Ω and 200Ω — same 1:2 ratio as 1k+2k, works perfectly.

⚠️

Common GND between Pi and L298N required  •  HC-SR04 ECHO needs 100Ω+200Ω voltage divider (5V → 3.3V)  •  Do NOT power Pi from Smart Car Board  •  Left motors parallel = Ch.A  •  Right = Ch.B  •  ENA/ENB jumpers on L298N: leave ON for full speed (or wire to PWM for speed control)

STOP — Read This Carefully

The HC-SR04 ECHO pin outputs 5 volts. Your Raspberry Pi GPIO pins can only handle 3.3 volts. Without a voltage divider, you will permanently damage your Pi.

Use your 100Ω + 200Ω resistors on the breadboard (same 1:2 ratio).
