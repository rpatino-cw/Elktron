Motor Wires 01–06
#LabelStick it onPreview
01LEFT FWDThe wire running from GPIO 17 to L298N IN1. Wrap around wire near the Pi end. This is the left-side forward signal.
02LEFT BWDThe wire from GPIO 27 to L298N IN2. Wrap near Pi end. Left-side reverse signal.
03RIGHT FWDWire from GPIO 22 to L298N IN3. Wrap near Pi end. Right-side forward.
04RIGHT BWDWire from GPIO 23 to L298N IN4. Wrap near Pi end. Right-side reverse.
05L298N LEFTStick on the OUT1/OUT2 wires leaving the L298N toward the left motor pair (FL + RL). These are the power output lines — not GPIO signal.
06L298N RIGHTStick on the OUT3/OUT4 wires leaving the L298N toward the right motor pair (FR + RR).
Sensor & Servo Wires 07–12
#LabelStick it onPreview
07ULTRA ECHOWire from HC-SR04 ECHO pin through the voltage divider (1kΩ + 2kΩ) to GPIO 24. Label the wire between the divider and the Pi — this is the safe 3.3V side.
08ULTRA TRIGWire from GPIO 25 directly to HC-SR04 TRIG pin. Label near the Pi end.
09SENSOR 5VThe 5V power wire from Pi Pin 2 to HC-SR04 VCC. Label near where it leaves the Pi header.
10SENSOR GNDThe ground wire from Pi Pin 9 to HC-SR04 GND. Label near the Pi end.
11PAN SERVOThe signal wire going to the pan (horizontal) servo on the pan/tilt platform. Connected to GPIO 12. Label where the wire meets the servo connector.
12TILT SERVOThe signal wire going to the tilt (vertical) servo. Connected to GPIO 13. Label at the servo connector end.
Power 13–14, 21–22
#LabelStick it onPreview
1318650 WIREThe positive and negative leads running from the 18650 battery holder to the Smart Car Board / L298N +12V input. Label both wires near the battery holder.
14COMMON GNDThe ground bridge wire between Pi GND (Pin 6) and L298N GND. This is critical — without it, GPIO signals won't reference correctly. Label both ends.
21PWR 18650Stick directly on the 18650 battery holder housing. Indicates this is the motor power source (7.4V).
22PWR BANKStick on or next to the USB-C power bank. Indicates this powers the Pi 5 (5V/3A). Place where it's visible when mounted on chassis.
Board Identification 15–20
#LabelStick it onPreview
15RASPBERRY PI 5Stick on any visible flat surface of the Pi 5 — top of the board near the edge, avoiding the GPIO header and heat sink. Should be readable when looking down at the chassis.
16L298NStick on the L298N motor driver board — the flat area next to the heat sink. Identifies the motor controller.
17HC-SR04Stick on the back of the ultrasonic sensor PCB, or on the chassis surface directly below the sensor mount. Identifies the range finder.
18DIVIDERStick near the voltage divider resistors (1kΩ + 2kΩ). If soldered on a small breadboard, label the breadboard. If inline, wrap around the junction. Critical safety component — 5V → 3.3V for ECHO pin.
19ARDUCAMStick on the camera ribbon cable near where it exits the Pi’s CSI port, or on the camera module housing if there’s space. Identifies the IMX708 wide-angle camera.
20PAN/TILTStick on the pan/tilt platform base — the Arducam B0283 bracket. Place where it won’t interfere with servo movement.
Orientation 23–26
#LabelStick it onPreview
23FRONTStick on the front edge of the chassis frame — the side the ultrasonic sensor faces. Arrow should point forward (direction of travel). This is the most important orientation label.
24LEFTStick on the left side rail of the chassis (left when looking from behind). Motor pair FL + RL is on this side.
25RIGHTStick on the right side rail. Motor pair FR + RR is on this side.
26CAM UPStick on the PVC mast near the top, with the arrow pointing upward. Indicates correct mast orientation so the camera faces the right direction after assembly.
Branding 27
#LabelStick it onPreview
27ELKTRONStick on the rear face of the chassis — the back panel or bottom plate rear edge. This is the name badge. Centered, level, visible when the bot drives away from you.
