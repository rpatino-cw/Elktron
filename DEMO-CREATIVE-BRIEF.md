## Step 1 — Pick a Story (or make your own)

These are different ways to frame the same project. Pick the one that feels right or combine pieces.

### A. "The Journey"
Start with parts on a table. End with a robot following someone down a DC aisle. It's a transformation story — raw components becoming something that moves and thinks.

*Opens with:* Close-up of screws, wires, Pi board, chassis pieces
*Ends with:* Bot driving autonomously, detection bounding boxes on screen
*Feels like:* A timelapse build video. Satisfying.

### B. "The Problem"
Start with the pain. A DCT walking a vendor around for an hour. Manual optic seating, one by one. Then show the fix — robots doing both jobs.

*Opens with:* "At CoreWeave, a technician spends 2 hours escorting one vendor."
*Ends with:* Before/after split screen — manual vs. robot
*Feels like:* A product pitch. Clear ROI.

### C. "The Heist"
Seven days. $430. A Raspberry Pi. Can we build two robots that actually work on the DC floor? Frame it like a challenge — will it work or won't it?

*Opens with:* Team huddle, shopping list, parts arriving
*Ends with:* The moment the wheels spin / the arm seats an optic
*Feels like:* A mini-documentary. Tension and payoff.

### D. "Day in the Life"
Follow a DCT through their shift. Show every repetitive task — escorting, seating optics, checking racks. Then introduce the robots as the solution.

*Opens with:* A tech clocking in, walking the floor
*Ends with:* Same tech, but now the robots handle the grunt work
*Feels like:* Relatable. Everyone at CW knows what DCTs do.

### E. "The Reveal"
Start with the finished product — bot following a person, arm seating an optic, dashboard showing everything live. Then rewind. "Here's how we got here."

*Opens with:* The coolest shot we have — robot in motion on the DC floor
*Ends with:* Team shot, "Built in 7 days by Team Elktron"
*Feels like:* A movie trailer. Hook first, context after.

## Step 2 — Pick a Tone

### Hype / Cinematic
Dark visuals. Bass music. Dramatic reveals. Text flying on screen. Product launch energy.
*Reference:* Apple product videos, Tesla unveils

### Documentary / Real
Handheld footage. Honest voiceover. Show the mess, the wires, the real DC floor. Authenticity wins.
*Reference:* Tested (YouTube), How It's Made

### Scrappy / Fun
Fast cuts. Personality. Maybe some humor. "We built a robot in a week and it actually works."
*Reference:* Stuff Made Here, hackathon highlight reels
